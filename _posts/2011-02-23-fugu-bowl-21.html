After nine days, Fugu Bowl 2.1 was approved on the App Store yesterday. Originally, this app was an experimental testbed for HyperBowl controls, but it’s continued in a guinea pig role. I think it’s the first app I made universal, when the iPad came out, and the in the last release I tried out iAds, using the plugin from Prime31 Studios. This latest update tried out Unity 3.2, two more Prime31 plugins (the Etcetera plugin for the Rate feature, and the Game Center plugin), and GUIKit001 from GameAssets.net for the pause menu.

Interestingly, Fugu Bowl is also my best “selling” app, with 75,000 non-upgrade downloads. I only just noticed on App Annie that the app has been included in “What’s Hot” in several non-US app stores. Maybe simple apps without a lot of non-localized text do better worldwide? (the far more complex HyperBowl app has most of its sales in the US and British commonwealth)
