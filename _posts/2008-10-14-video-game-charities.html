Last week I dropped off my annual donation to Get Well Gamers, a charity founded by Ryan Sharpe that supplies video games as a pain management tool for children recovering in hospitals. They’re located here in Huntington Beach, so it’s a convenient drive-by for me, I unload all the video games I’ll never get around to playing (everything that’s not the latest generation), and I could use the tax break (it’ll make a tiny dent in what I lost in home and stock market value this year).

A google search for other video game charities proved disappointing, listing more charity events than sustaining charity organizations, but I did find a few that looked promising, e.g. the Gamers Outreach Foundation and another one for hospital-bound children, Child’s Play. In an industry not exactly known for maturity, it’s encouraging to see some do-gooding.
