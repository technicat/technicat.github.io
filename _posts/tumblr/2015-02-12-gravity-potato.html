You might think our planet has a uniform gravitational field - but you’d be wrong! There’s actually quite a bit of variation (though at such small scales we’d never be able to feel it). Using a suite of satellites, the people at the German Research Center for Geosciences created this “Gravity Potato” map of the earth. Red areas that swell outwards = higher gravity. Bluer areas that dip inward = lower gravity.
